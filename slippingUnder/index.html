Slipping

Your body is pulled into a tight ball. Every muscle is tensed and ready. Your eyes are pressed tightly together but a tear squeezes through and rolls slowly downward. You tuck your head into your chest.

Against your back you can feel the other leaning against you.

They must be asleep. You are alone.

You lay, very quiet but very awake. You hope the other does not notice. That they do not feel the quick motion of your lungs or smell your fear. They can never know the truth.

This is the first law of blind faith. You must never tell a soul.

You consider, why are you afraid?

No, not why. There is no reason, no logic.

This feeling is too wild to be defined, to be held down. Your thoughts turn to the sky.

You fear the sunset.

You fear, the ending of the day. The coming of night. You fear sleep, silence, absence of thought. You fear the knock at the door. You fear the light in the tunnel.

You fear there is an end.

You stay at the table. You sit, you do not move or speak. You stare straight down and hope no one notices.

They cannot understand. They would judge you and tell you that you cannot not do anything to change it so why worry, as if you did not already know.

They would tell you that is just the way it works, and that you have to accept it.

But you have tried to accept it. You have told yourself that everything is ok. You never really believe yourself.

This is the second law of blind faith. Always be afraid.

You want to stand and fight for your life but your heart is gone and in its place there is a void that saps your energy and pulls you down.

But that doesn't matter, because there is no winning this fight..

All you can feel is the dread.. You tell yourself that your time will never come.

But, of course, it will.

It's not as if you don't search for answers. Like you don't search every minute for an answer to your fear.

You despise your helplessness. You despise your weakness.

But no matter how much you rage or cry, no matter how much you curse your own name, you cannot alter the nature of your being.

This is the third law of blind faith. You can never change.

Why even bother?

Is it not easier to turn to a pipe? To suppress your mind so that there is no thought strong enough to reach you?

There are no saviors, no hope in here.

You rocket down the highway. You are afraid if you slowed you might lose too much momentum so you press your foot down on the gas and shut your eyes.

You stay at the bottom of the pit.
